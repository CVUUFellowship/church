Finances / Stewardship

 

Members will be expected to contribute responsibly, as they are able, to sustain the annual operating budget, to fund our intermediary needs (such as capital campaigns) and insure that our long term needs are met by funding an endowment.

The Church budget for the current fiscal year is $750,000. This is an increase of $300,000 from just five years ago. As in years past, approximately 75% of the budget is generated from members and pledging friends. The total amount pledged has increased 56% from 5 years ago. This helps us in making informed projections for the years to come. It’s important to mention that in the past year, in addition to funding Neighborhood’s on-going mission with our pledges and gifts, Church members also contributed $150,000 to fund the furniture & fixtures fund. That means that Church members and friends raised approximately $700,000 in direct

contributions.

 

In five years we believe the annual Church budget will be 1.2 million dollars. To fund the programs in the current year’s budget in five years will require a budget of $916,000 (based on the current budget times a 4% increase each year for five -years). On the revenue side, $986,000 will be realized in pledges (assuming an increase of 8% per year for the next five years). If we continue the trend that pledges make up 75% of our budget then we know that the other $200,000 plus will be realized through other sources of income. Simple math tells us that in excess of $316,000 per year will be available to fund many of the staffing, maintenance and program needs that we outline in the rest of this report.

 

A culture of stewardship will need to be continually nurtured to support the vision of the church.

 

During the next five years we will want to:

  • Educate the congregation on the various vehicles for stewardship in a simplified way i.e. bequests and stock gifts.
  • Formalize a planned giving program.
  • Direct our Senior Minister to take a more active role in developing possible income sources.
  • Research and develop a grant development component of fund raising.
  • Maintain a positive relationship with renters and evaluate the rental schedule annually.
  • Recognize all those contributing to the life of the church whether through time, talent or money in a systematic and thoughtful manner.
  • Complete a successful capital campaign and begin planning a new one.
