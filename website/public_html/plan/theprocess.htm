The Process:

 

 

In the fall of 1999 the Board of Trustees, after much discussion, approved a broad vision for the immediate future of Neighborhood Church. This document formed the basis for the decision to create a Strategic Planning Committee. The committee was made up of three current Board members and four non-board members. Their charge was to formulate a plan that would project what Neighborhood Church would “look like” in five years. The Committee outlined seven areas that they would concentrate on. They are: membership, connection/community, worship, children/youth & their families, buildings & grounds, staff, community outreach, finances/stewardship.

 

In gathering information and resources the Committee organized and invited members and friends to twenty home gatherings. Members of the Board of Trustees facilitated the gatherings where the question “what will Neighborhood Church look tike in five years?" was pondered and discussed. Each facilitator forwarded a written report of their findings to the committee. The feedback from these gatherings formed the basis of much of the committee’s discussion and ultimately its recommendations. In addition, similar discussions were held with the Board of Trustees, and staff and various committees.

 

It is important to note that this report should still be considered a draft and work in progress. Although the report represents many hours of thought and reflection by committee members it is not to be considered the final word. After a review by the Board of Trustees it will be submitted to church committees and the congregation at large for further study and refinements. Ultimately, it is recommended that the congregation be given the opportunity to pass or reject the plan in its entirety.
