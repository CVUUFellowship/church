Children, Youth and Their Families

 

Our commitment now must be to fill that space with opportunities for our families to worship, learn and play together in groups both large and small

We shall broaden and deepen our wellspring by providing:

 

A wide offering of educational opportunities for both children and adults, that will enable us to explore the world of faith and religion, to support each other in daily living, and to create a more just and compassionate world

 

We are a church that presently attracts families with children. Our present Religious Education Sunday morning programming enrollment is approximately 200 children and youth, although we frequently have more children attending then are actually registered. The number is higher if you consider all the children and youth involved in Sunday and during the week programs. With our programs building we have a wonderful and welcoming new physical home for our families. As we deepen the bonds of our church family we must also expand and broaden our reach into the community that surrounds us, lending a helping hand in the work of living and helping to create a more just and compassionate world.

 

During the next five years we will have more opportunities for families to worship together (see worship). We will need to explore ways for families to participate in the life of the church.

 

These might include:

·       Family based outreach activities.

·       Develop an outward focus that brings in more families from the immediate neighborhood of the church and that will be more reflective (racially, socially, economically, politically etc.) of all the communities from which we draw families.

·       Structured and unstructured family recreation time.

·       More opportunities for people of different ages to make both choral and instrumental music together.

·       To challenge and honor our youth, we will provide support that will enable them to initiate and carry forward outreach programs that serve the needs of the larger community. By providing support, mentoring and encouragement we will help them to travel the many paths to the spirit and to share that experience with their peers.

 

To continue to broaden and deepen our educational offerings we will continue to seek out and develop curricula for religious education at all age levels, helping children to explore the world of faith and religion, helping teens and young adults to come of age with honor, and helping parents to nurture and sustain growing families as they continue on their spiritual path. We will continue to use multiple learning models to reach an increasingly diverse group of learners and leaders.

 

We will seek to renovate and expand our recreational facilities on campus to provide a safe play area for our youngest learners and additional recreation for our older youth
