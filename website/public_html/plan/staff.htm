Staff

 

“As a rule of thumb, if you desire to staff for growth, you need one fulltime program person on your staff for every one hundred active members”

According to the book, The In-Between Church, by Alice Mann (an Alban Institute publication) “growing churches face a dilemma. They don’t have enough households to fully support a new staff person, but they will never gain those households without additional staff energy. In general, a church must staff somewhat ahead of the immediate need in order to keep growing? In the same book Roy Oswald, a senior consultant at the Institute, is quoted as follows, “As a rule of thumb, if you desire to staff for growth, you need one full-time program person on your staff for every one hundred active members” (usually based on average attendance).... You are staffing for maintenance if you are just slightly under that figure. You are staffed for decline if you are seriously under that figure? The book clarifies “This one-per-hundred rule assumes that you already have the basic services of a church musician, secretarial support and janitorial support. At this time we have 3 full time program professionals and are considered under-staffed. We are grossly understaffed if we continue to grow.

 

In Neighborhood’s case we employ:

  • A full-time Senior Minister
  • A full-time Minister of Religious Education
  • A full-time Director of Music
  • A full-time Administrator
  • A part-time Director of Outreach (10 hours per week).
  • Various support staff such as office assistant, janitor, RE assistant, childcare workers etc.

 

It will be difficult to project exactly when to hire additional staff and in what order. However, the goal should be to stay ahead of the need. Obviously, salaries of our professional staff have a major impact on the annual budget. The Board of Trustees should direct the Senior Minister and the Personnel Committee to make recommendations each year as to what positions should be funded. Within the next five years we anticipate that there would be:

  • A full time Director of Outreach who would provide leadership and expand the number of opportunities for outreach.
  • A Coordinator of Volunteers who would facilitate participation of members and friends in outreach opportunities, committee work, and administrate new members programs.
  • A third professional who would lead non-traditional services (see worship)
  • A Small Group Coordinator who would develop and coordinate intentional small groups for the congregation.
  • A Sunday Resource Person. Since Sunday mornings are a time when the most members are on the campus, a staff member or trained volunteer would be on-site to coordinate activities, answer questions etc. on Sunday mornings.
  • A Director of Development who would attract grants & fellowships, and coordinate pledge/gift programs.
  • Funding for additional support staff so our professional staff can operate efficiently.
