Worship

 

This is the time to think about an additional weekly, more participatory service offered between the two traditional Sunday services.

We shall broaden and deepen our wellspring by providing:

 

An excellence In worship which focuses mystery and intellect into one and offers spiritual renewal to all seekers~

 

A culture which is enhanced by the arts, especially music, for the arts are creations of the spirit.

 

Worship is the heart of the religious and spiritual life of Neighborhood Church. We have a long history of quality preaching and worship services. The purpose of worship is to offer people a glimpse of the universe’s wholeness that we hope to reach. Through the liberal rituals of music and word, in the company of the religious community, each is offered an opportunity for spiritual and religious renewal. Although the traditional forms of worship speak to the largest contingent of people, there are less traditional modes that speak to others. Currently, adults attend one of two Sunday services offered. In addition, children, youth and their Religious Education leaders regularly attend Sunday Chapel Worship.

 

People have come to expect worship services of the highest quality at Neighborhood Church. To this end the vision for worship is that we will endeavor to improve the quality of the worship experience for everyone by being mindful of the details, including ushering, sound and lighting. The aesthetics of our worship space will be enhanced (see Building & Grounds). The music will continue to improve in excellence, participation and variety.

 

During the next five years we will:

  • Add a third, weekly, more participatory service, held between the two traditional Sunday services. It will be organized by a professional who is accountable for its effectiveness (not necessarily a minister, but accountable to a minister). It will be a multigenerational service and it will have a variety of excellent music available to it.

 

  • Add a fourth, weekly service that takes place outside Sunday mornings. It will be participatory in structure. It will be organized by a professional who is accountable for its effectiveness (not necessarily a minister, but accountable to a minister). It will have a variety of excellent music available to it.

 

Keeping in mind that worship is at the heart of spiritual life at Neighborhood Church, committees and small group gatherings might initiate each meeting with a brief period of worship. This could take the form of chalice lighting or a reading, but could offer people the opportunity to reflect and center upon the purposes for which they are gathered.
