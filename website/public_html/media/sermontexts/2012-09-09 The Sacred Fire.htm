The Sacred Fire

©Rev. Lora Brandis

Preached September 9, 2012

Conejo Valley Unitarian Universalist Congregation

 

This church year we are igniting a sacred fire dedicated to exploring a full range of theological themes both in worship and in religious education. Starting next week we’ll begin a shared examination of the Reverend Tom Owen-Towle’s book Theology Ablaze with a series of theological themes that will inspire our worship, inform all of our religious education and spark a flame of inquiry in our hearts and minds as we journey together this church year.

It all begins now as we return from our summer travels and our summer sabbaths, we ask each other, “What have you brought to this sanctuary today to preserve the flame that burns brightly on this hearth, even when we are far away?” Passing the flame from candle to candle and heart to heart we are affirming the mission of this congregation as we start a new year. We are, in a paraphrase of John O’Donohue’s words, discovering beneath our fear the embers of anger to kindle justice. We are breathing “new urgency into our love of life” and allowing the “flame of passion to burn away what is false.” We are proclaiming and praying, “May courage cause our lives to flame.” (“In Praise of Fire”)

And so we say together, “May courage cause our lives to flame.”

Even now a volcano in Nicaragua is expelling an ash cloud into the atmosphere, causing hundreds of nearby residents to be evacuated. A fire that broke out last Sunday afternoon in the Angeles National Forest is just now nearly contained. Fires in Colorado this summer caused devastating damage that will take years to rebuild. Fire is a powerful element. We invoke it with great care.

The fire of the big bang lives on in the earth’s core that rumbles and tumbles out in the ash cloud of a volcano. The heat of our bodies connects us to this original fire. The fireball that is the sun travels to us at a great distance, cooling down on its way so that life can be sustained on our tiny blue-green planet.  The first humans harnessed the power of fire, some worshipped it and named it as a god. Fire is a powerful element. We invoke it with great care.

Our ancestors gathered around fires made sacred by shared stories. The stories in turn became sacred as they carried the evolving wisdom arising out of the collective heartache of wandering tribes. As people moved into settlements they brought the fire inside to the hearth, the center of family life. Irish poet and philosopher John O’Donohue tells us that some family fires have never been extinguished. Through a practice called “smooring” live embers are kept aflame beneath a layer of ash. From generation to generation, the hearth remains ablaze. From generation to generation, the sacred fire lights up the darkness.

We gather today, coming home to this sanctuary, where we light a chalice each Sunday reminding ourselves of the ancient symbol of the flame burning in the temple, the lamps burning on altars, the sacred bonfires burning in the wilderness. How will we keep our sacred fire ablaze together this year? How will we honor the live embers of our faith that has been passed from generation to generation? Coming to this place where our heart is at home, returning to this intentional community of spiritually courageous people, we say together “May courage cause our lives to flame.”

“May courage cause our lives to flame.” The element of fire invites us to face our fears of irrevocable loss, devastating humiliation and the painful judgment of others.

“May courage cause our lives to flame.” The desire to be loved blazes in our hearts. The desire to be heard, to speak our truth without fear of reprisal, to stand up for what is right, burns brightly.

“May courage cause our lives to flame.” We need the fire of courage to love ourselves boldly, to sift through the ashes of what is false for that one spark of truth, and to endure with strength the trials and tribulations of a life willing to be formed by the sacred fire.

“May courage cause our lives to flame.” Seeking always for more awareness of the holy fire within, seeking and doubting, having the courage to live the question, living in the “I don’t know” of life, we say together:

“May courage cause our lives to flame.” Trusting in a sacred passion that calls us to transform the world through justice and compassion, we say together:

May courage cause our lives to flame.

Being passionately ablaze with the life force that fuels transformation, we say together one last time:

May courage cause our lives to flame.

As we engage in our final ritual, I will bring the light of the chalice to you. Share it with each other. Share your courage with each other. Re-light each other’s flames, keeping the candles burning through the final hymn.
