CHARTER QUESTIONAIRRE

 

1)      What is the formal name of your group, committee, or key contributor? 

Aesthetics Committee (“F Troop”)

 

2)      Describe the key purpose(s) of your group or committee in two or three sentences.  (Describe what you are trying to accomplish in terms of goals rather than how you will accomplish them.)

The purpose of the committee is to provide guidelines and a review process for furnishings used in CVUUF buildings, including donations and requests by groups for additional items.

 

3)      Describe in one or two sentences how your group fits within the Fellowship’s organization.  (For example, “The Outreach Group is established by the Board of Trustees and is one of the six principal operating Groups of the Fellowship.”)

The Aesthetics Committee is established by the Board of Trustees as an ad-hoc resource, and provides guidance to existing groups and individual members of the Fellowship.

 

4)      Describe how your group or committee leader (e.g. Group Director or Chair) is chosen.  (For example, “The Group Director is appointed by the Board of Trustees” or “The Chair of the Committee is elected by the Committee members”).

The Committee operates as a consensus group, with chairmanship duties (minutes and agendas) rotating as needed. Any review required will be undertaken by the full committee.

 

5)      What is the term of service for your leader?  Not applicable.  What is the term of service of your members? 

 

6)      Describe any limits on the number of members in your Group or Committee (if there are none, specify as such).

Five members.  Committee members will be confirmed by the Board of Trustees.

 

7)      If the Group or Committee will make regular reports to another governing body, please specify the identity of the body.   For example, the Group Director will make regular reports to the Council of Directors or “the Committee chair will make regular reports to the [Group] or [Group Director].

No regular reports specified.

8)      Specify how often your Group or Committee will meet (For example, once a month, once a quarter or as needed if that is the case).

Committee will meet as required.

9)      Specify the key duties and responsibilities of your Group or Committee.  (In this section, you should list specific activities that your Group or Committee undertakes, such as planning worship services, reserving facilities etc.)

 

·     Development of a “Style” book, containing palettes and guidelines used in decisions on fittings and furnishings during the build-out process.

·     Development of guidelines for end-users and potential donors

·     Review of donations as offered to the Fellowship. Donations that require committee approval will have full committee review.

·     Review of requests for additional furniture or changes made by end-users.

·     Development of policy (for board approval) on furnishings guidelines.

 

 

Aesthetics Committee (F Troop)

 

Suggestions for guidelines:

 

·   Anything attached to the building requires approval

o       EXCEPTION: RE Classrooms may hang curriculum related posters and materials, committee will provide guidelines for how to hang (no thumb tacks, etc)

 

·   Common-area furniture requires approval

 

·   Accessories and work-specific items should be managed by the committee chairs who use them, or group directors if appropriate.

 

·   Nothing may be left at site for donation - any item must be reviewed by the responsible party, and either accepted or denied.

 

·   If the item requires aesthetics committee approval, a review will be undertaken by the full committee. Authority not to be delegated to single committee members.

 

·   Items may be submitted for review by the committee by emailing ftroop@cvuuf.org

 

·   Items that are left on site without approval will be disposed of

 

  • Items that may have been accepted but which have not been stored and are taking up public space will be disposed of.
