MINUTES OF REGULAR MEETING OF THE

BOARD OF TRUSTEES OF THE

UNITARIAN UNIVERSALIST FELLOWSHIP OF THE CONEJO VALLEY

 

June 19, 2007

 

            A regular meeting of the Board of Trustees of the Unitarian Universalist Fellowship of the Conejo Valley (the “Fellowship”) was held on Tuesday, June 19, 2007 at 7:30 p.m. at our temporary conference room in Thousand Oaks.  In attendance at the meeting were Jeff Goldstein, Bill Robinson, Paul Harrison, Laura McLean, Susan Bow, Jill Schlauss representing the Multiple Service Task Force and Claudia Barton who acted as Secretary.   Jim Bray, Rev. Betty and Paul Chavez were unable to attend.

 

 

            Jeff opened the meeting and Bill conducted the worship moment. 

 

 

Minutes and Agenda

 

            The meeting agenda was reviewed and approved.  The minutes of the prior meeting held in May were reviewed and approved.  The minutes from the April meeting were also reviewed and approved.

 

 

Worship Service Expansion Report – Jill Schlauss

 

Jill presented the report created by the Worship Service Expansion Taskforce, along with the member feedback received to date.  The Board reviewed the report and the findings and discussed them in depth.  The Taskforce will be reconvening to determine the best process for implementing multiple services, however, a timeframe has not been established.  There was much discussion about when we might begin multiple services.  It was decided more feedback from the congregation is needed before this can be addressed.  Also, a communication plan still needs to be developed so we are sure to get feedback from as many members as possible.

 

 

Executive Session

 

            Because of the sensitive nature of a congregational issue and in order to ensure that the Trustees felt comfortable in freely expressing their ideas and exploring solutions to the problem, the board voted unanimously, to enter Executive Session and not formally record detailed minutes of the deliberations.

 

 

Complaint and Conflict Resolution Procedure – Susan Bow & Paul Harrison

 

            Susan and Paul explained that it would be most effective to have these as two separate procedures or policies.  Establishing a balanced committee to hear complaints might be a challenge.  Time limits for the committee members were discussed.  It was mentioned that time limits might be difficult, depending on how many volunteers we have to choose from.  It was agreed that being part of this committee might be exhausting.  Due to the length of time the Executive Session used, it was agreed to continue this at next month’s meeting.  Paul has agreed to continue working with Susan on this project even though this is his last meeting.

 

            We would like to formally thank Paul for his years of service.  He is a valued member of our congregation and has served faithfully on the Board.  Thank you, Paul.

 

 

Council Report – Bill Robinson

 

            The Council spent much time discussing volunteerism and how we can improve recruiting and appreciation efforts for our volunteers.  Each director will be recreating the Charters for their various committees.  At each meeting, the council will review the charters for one or two directors in order to refine the process and discuss the benefits of creating the charters. 

 

 

Site Taskforce – Bill Robinson

 

            The foundation is poured on the fellowship building and will be soon on the sanctuary.  Monday, June 25 is the meeting at the planning commission.  We have several members and Rev. Betty ready to speak in support of the SUP (special use permit.)  It is not anticipated that there will be any objections to the permit, however, the meeting is televised and this may be a good opportunity for additional exposure for our fellowship.

 

 

Finance Report – Jeff Goldstein

 

            The Board members all reviewed the financial in advance of the meeting and there were no questions. 

            The appointed of Peter Schlauss as the Chair of the Stewardship
Committee was moved, seconded and unanimously approved.

 

 

 

Board Meeting Reporters – Claudia Barton

 

            There have been a couple of requests from members to have more details on the actual discussions held at the Board meetings.  It was uniformly agreed that the minutes are not the appropriate place for such details, as they are a reflection of the business conducted by the board and a working tool for the board.  It was discussed whether the Board should invite members to attend the board meetings and write about their experiences as observers.  It was decided that this would be a time consuming and difficult process for the board to undertake at this time.  If there were a volunteer who wanted to do this, it would need to be treated as a journalistic endeavor and therefore should include board member review of articles to guarantee accuracy of topics and quotes.

 

 

Adjournment

 

There being no further business to come before the Board, it was, upon motion duly made and seconded, unanimously adjourned at 10:17 p.m.

 

                                                                        Respectfully submitted,
